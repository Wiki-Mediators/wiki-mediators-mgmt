# Decision Graphs With Battle Edges

## The idea (operator's framing, 2026-07-03)

A TensorFlow-style flow chart of the project, except the edges are not
learned weights — they are stickmen battling over ideas. Resolved edges
render as quiet settled lines. UNRESOLVED edges host a live battle: the
"undecided layer." Many branches, many battles. The weight of an edge is
who is currently winning.

## Why it is grounded, not just cute

- The vault already encodes decision state: booked-vs-built status,
  triggers fired-or-not, the parked research fork (stop / new market /
  relax the OOS seal), open management questions 1–7, screen verdicts.
- An animated edge is a preattentive signal: where battles run, attention
  debt lives. A glance at the graph answers "what is still undecided?"
  faster than reading the roadmap.
- Distinct from the edge-status stickmen (which render STATE); this
  renders INDECISION. Complementary, same rig.

## Grounded examples of battle edges (from real vault state)

- research fork: three-way battle, parked by choice — the flagship bout
- sweeper trigger: "fired" vs "not fired" (usage evidence says fired;
  operator has not ruled)
- range levels vs swing levels: screen already scored it 58–8 by count
  but −$304 vs +$543 by net — a battle the crowd would argue about
- every 6.x roadmap entry: a fighter on a bench attached to its trigger

## Obsidian feasibility (honest notes)

- Obsidian Canvas can arrange nodes and embed notes/SVGs, but canvas
  edges themselves cannot be animated.
- Workable path: a dumb deriver reads roadmap/altitude/decision state and
  emits ONE animated SVG per undecided edge (SMIL animation — plays in
  Obsidian preview with no plugins) plus a generated dashboard note that
  lays out nodes and embeds the edge SVGs between them. Deterministic,
  regenerated, zero hand-keeping.
- Resolution event: when the operator decides, the deriver's next run
  renders that edge as a settled line with the loser gone to the void —
  the graph's history of finished battles IS the decision log, derived.
