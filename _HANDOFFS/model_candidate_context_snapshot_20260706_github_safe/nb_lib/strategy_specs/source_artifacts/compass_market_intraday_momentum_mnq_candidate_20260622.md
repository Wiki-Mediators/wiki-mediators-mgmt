Market Intraday Momentum on MNQ: A Buildable, Pre-Committed Strategy Candidate
TL;DR

The effect is real, well-documented, and structurally fits MNQ's needs (once-daily, ~250 trades/year, hold-into-close move of ~40–75 index points dwarfs the ~2-point round-trip cost), BUT the Nasdaq-100 is the weakest documented vehicle out-of-sample (Gao et al.'s QQQ had in-sample R²=1.43% but an out-of-sample R² of just 0.70%, the lowest of ten ETFs tested), at least one peer-reviewed replication (Rosa 2022) finds the predictability disappears out-of-sample, and a 2026 MNQ-specific falsification study found related intraday signals fail institutional validation after costs.
Recommended build: an unconditional, parameter-locked candidate — compute r1 (9:30→10:00 ET return measured from the prior day's close), enter at 15:30 ET in the direction of sign(r1), exit flat by 15:58:30 ET. Add the r12 (15:00–15:30) confirmation as a documented variant. Treat volatility/volume/FOMC conditioning as labels recorded for post-hoc analysis, not as gates, to protect the ~20-trades/month power floor.
This is a hypothesis to test on your own sealed data, not a validated edge. Pre-commit go/no-go thresholds (after-cost profit factor, per-trade edge in points, best-day concentration, per-year stability) before unsealing the out-of-sample window. Expect a coin-flip-plus win rate (~51–55%) with mild positive skew. The honest base-rate expectation from the literature is that decayed or regime-dependent performance is the most likely outcome on MNQ.

Key Findings
1. The canonical effect. Gao, Han, Li & Zhou (2018, Journal of Financial Economics 129(2):394–414), using SPY data from 1993–2013, show that the first half-hour return r1 (measured from the prior day's close to 10:00 ET) positively predicts the last half-hour return r13 (15:30–16:00 ET). Per the paper, r1 "positively predicts the last half-hour return, r13, with a scaled (by 100) slope of 6.94, statistically significant at the 1% level, and an R2 of 1.6%"; combining r1 with the twelfth half-hour return r12 (15:00–15:30) raises in-sample R² to 2.6%. Out-of-sample R² was 1.4% (r1 alone) and 2.0% (combined). A sign(r1) market-timing strategy earned 6.67%/yr at 6.19% volatility → Sharpe 1.08, versus 0.29 for daily buy-and-hold. super + 3
2. The gamma-hedging extension. Baltussen, Da, Lammers & Martens (2021, JFE 142(1):377–403) extend the result to "over 60 futures on equities, bonds, commodities, and currencies between 1974 and 2020," showing the "rest-of-day" return (rROD, prior close to the last 30 minutes) predicts the last-30-minute return everywhere. Per the paper, "a simple market intraday momentum trading strategy produces consistent returns over time, translating into high and attractive (annualized) Sharpe ratios between 0.87 and 1.73 at the asset class level." They attribute the effect to options-dealer and leveraged-ETF gamma hedging concentrated near the close, and show the last-30-minute move reverts over the next three days — consistent with transitory price pressure (hedging), not permanent information impact. ScienceDirect + 3
3. Conditioning amplifies the signal. Gao et al. document R² rising with volatility and volume, during recessions, and on macro-news days. The effect is concentrated on positive-r1 days: on positive-r1 financial-crisis days "the R2s increase to 4.5%, 11.0%, and 14.1%, respectively, for the three predictive regressions," whereas on negative-r1 days the R²s are only "0.7%, 0.0%, and 0.9%." Separately, FOMC-minutes-release days showed R² of 11.0% (vs 2.5% on non-release days) with timing returns near 20%/yr. The asymmetry (much stronger when the morning move is up and large) is a core feature. Mql5super
4. The Nasdaq-specific caveat. For QQQ specifically (Gao et al. Table 12), in-sample timing return was 7.75%/yr (t=3.65) and in-sample R²=1.43%, but the out-of-sample R² was only 0.70% — the lowest of all ten ETFs tested. The multivariate regression gave a QQQ r1 coefficient of 5.67 (t=3.79) and r12 of 9.76 (t=2.59), total R²=3.90%. This OOS weakness is the single most important robustness flag for an MNQ application.
5. The skeptical literature. Rosa (2022, Journal of Futures Markets 42:2218–2234) finds the intraday-momentum predictability disappears entirely out-of-sample, attributes the disappearance (rather than mere decay) to data mining, and shows via a Markov-switching model that any predictability is confined to a high-signal-strength regime — concluding a threshold strategy beats an always-on one. A 2026 MNQ-specific falsification study (Mesfin, arXiv:2605.04004) tested 14 intraday signal families on 947 days of 5-minute MNQ data (2021–2025) and found none passed institutional validation (min t-stat 2.0, ≥30 OOS trades, positive net after a 2-point round-trip cost, multi-year stability); gross edge for OHLCV signals was just 0.07–1.50 points/trade. More broadly, McLean & Pontiff (2016, Journal of Finance 71(1):5–32) found across 97 predictors that "portfolio returns are 26% lower out-of-sample and 58% lower post-publication." arXivWiley Online Library
Details
Predictor-and-trade structure (exact windows)

r1 (signal): r1,t = (price at 10:00 ET / prior-day 16:00 ET close) − 1. Gao et al. deliberately anchor to the previous close so r1 captures overnight + opening-30-minute information (most macro releases hit at 8:30 ET, before the open). A pure 9:30→10:00 RTH-open variant is a legitimate alternative — document which you use, since results are definition-sensitive. super
r12 (optional confirmation): the 15:00→15:30 ET half-hour return. Gao et al. show it is independent of and complementary to r1 (combined R² ≈ sum of the individual R²s), and that only r1 and r12 (of the twelve prior half-hours) consistently predict r13. supersuper
Entry: at 15:30 ET, take a position in the direction of sign(r1) — or sign(r1) confirmed by sign(r12).
Exit: at the close. For MNQ, flatten by ~15:58:30 ET to avoid closing-auction illiquidity. Note Baltussen et al. found predictability does not extend past the 16:00 ET cash close (the futures settle at 16:15 but the edge is gone by 16:00) — a point that supports the hedging mechanism over informed-trading. nd
Variants in the literature: (a) sign(r1) only; (b) sign(r12) only; (c) trade only when r1 and r12 agree, else stay flat (Gao et al.'s "third strategy"). Baltussen et al. prefer rROD (the whole rest-of-day return) over r1 alone as the predictor in futures. superAlpha Architect

Magnitude / threshold question
Predictability is stronger when |r1| is large and when r1 is positive (combined-predictor R² ~4.5% on positive-r1 days vs ~0.9% on negative-r1 days). Rosa (2022) reinforces this: a threshold strategy that trades only on strong signals outperforms an always-on strategy. This justifies testing an optional |r1| threshold — but every threshold cuts trade count, in direct tension with the power requirement below. The gamma-hedging mechanism is intrinsically size-dependent: larger intraday moves force larger dealer re-hedging into the close, so a magnitude filter is mechanistically sensible even if it costs frequency.
Cost-distance and expected move

MNQ specs (CME Group): the Micro contract is $2 × the Nasdaq-100 index (the standard E-mini NQ is "$20 x the Nasdaq-100 index and has a minimum tick of 0.25 index points"). For MNQ: 1 tick = 0.25 index pt = $0.50; 1 full point = $2. Round-trip cost ≈ 1–1.25 pts (~$2–2.50) including commission plus one tick of slippage each side. CME GroupCME Group
Expected last-30-minute move: proxies indicate the Nasdaq-100 closing half-hour absolute move is on the order of ~0.2–0.35% (QQQ's opening 30 minutes average ±0.25% absolute, and the close is comparably elevated under the well-documented U-shaped intraday volatility pattern). At a ~20,000–22,000 index level that is ~40–75 index points (~$80–$150 per MNQ contract). For daily context, BrokerChooser (mid-2025) notes "the daily range of the Nasdaq 100 typically runs between 0.88% and 1.99% of the index... movements between 200 and 460 points being totally normal." Conclusion: the captured close-window move (tens of points) dwarfs the ~2-point round-trip cost by roughly 20–35×, easily satisfying a cost-distance requirement. This is the strategy's biggest structural advantage over scalping signals (Mesfin's failed MNQ signals captured only 0.07–1.5 gross points — they failed precisely on cost-distance).
Stop-loss: the canonical strategy is unconditional hold-to-close with no stop. Adding a stop truncates the right tail of a mildly positive-skewed payoff and converts the bet into something the literature never tested. Recommendation: backtest the pure no-stop version first as the registered hypothesis; test only a wide, catastrophe-only stop (e.g., scaled to daily range) as a separate, labeled variant.

Frequency and power
Firing once per RTH day = ~21 trades/month = ~250/year — roughly 10× a 20/month floor. Reaching ~950 trades (a Minimum-Track-Record-Length benchmark for establishing a modest edge) takes under 4 years of data. This frequency is the strategy's key statistical advantage. Therefore conditioning filters that cut frequency (FOMC-only ≈ 8 days/yr; high-vol-tercile ≈ 80 days/yr) are generally NOT worth applying as gates — they would destroy the power advantage that makes the candidate attractive in the first place. Record them as labels and analyze edge-by-regime post hoc instead.
Payoff skew / concentration
Gao et al. report the sign(r1) timing strategy had skewness ~0.38, kurtosis ~15.7, and a success rate ~50.9% — i.e., a near-coin-flip win rate carried by a modest positive mean and fat tails, with high-volatility/crisis days contributing disproportionately. This is less extreme than classic trend-following but still has tail concentration: excluding the 2007–2009 crisis, SPY in-sample R² fell from 1.6% to 0.8%. For a screening gate that rejects strategies where one day dominates net profit, expect concentration risk from a handful of high-volatility/FOMC days — so quantify the best-day and best-20-day profit shares explicitly. super + 2
Decay and robustness — the honest case

For the effect: Two top-tier finance-journal publications; a plausible, still-operative microstructure mechanism (gamma hedging has grown with 0DTE options and leveraged ETFs since 2018); Baltussen et al.'s mechanism implies persistence while dealer short-gamma persists.
Against: (i) Rosa (2022) — predictability disappears OOS, attributed to data mining; (ii) Gao et al.'s own QQQ OOS R² of 0.70% is the weakest of ten ETFs, making Nasdaq the shakiest vehicle; (iii) Asia-Pacific replications find the effect weaker post-COVID; (iv) general anomaly decay (McLean & Pontiff: returns 26% lower OOS, 58% lower post-publication); (v) Mesfin (2026) found no MNQ intraday signal family passed institutional validation 2021–2025 after costs. Balance: a plausibly-live but materially-decayed effect that must be proven on your own MNQ data, not assumed.

Recommendations
Stage 1 — In-sample design (registered hypothesis, locked before unsealing OOS):

Instrument: MNQ continuous front-month, RTH only, 1-minute bars (1-second for execution realism at the 15:30 entry and 15:58:30 exit).
Signal: r1 = (10:00 ET price / prior 16:00 ET close) − 1.
Direction: long if r1 > 0, short if r1 < 0.
Entry: 15:30:00 ET. Exit: flat by 15:58:30 ET.
Threshold: baseline = none (trade every day). Pre-register exactly ONE alternative: |r1| ≥ 0.25%.
Conditioning: none as a gate; record first-30-minute realized-volatility tercile, volume tercile, and FOMC/CPI/GDP day flags as labels for post-hoc analysis only.
Size: 1 MNQ contract (constant) for signal validation; defer position sizing until the edge is confirmed.
Cost model: 1.25 pts ($2.50) round-trip per contract (commission + 1-tick slippage each side). Stress-test at 2.0 pts.
In-sample window: earliest reliable MNQ/NQ RTH data through a frozen cutoff; seal the most recent ~30% as out-of-sample.

Pre-committed go/no-go metrics (evaluate in-sample, then confirm OOS at the SAME thresholds):

After-cost profit factor ≥ 1.15.
Per-trade edge ≥ 1.0 point net (must clear the ~1.25-pt cost with margin).
Trades/month ≥ 20 (baseline passes easily; the threshold variant must still clear it).
Best-day net-profit share < ~10–15%; best-20-day share modest (concentration gate).
Positive net P&L in ≥ 3 of 4 calendar years (year-stability — the exact criterion Mesfin's signals failed).
Win rate is not a gate (expect ~51–55%); the edge is mean × frequency, not hit rate.

Stage 2 — If in-sample passes: test the r1+r12-agreement variant and the |r1|≥0.25% threshold variant as separate registered hypotheses (do not optimize across them). Then unseal OOS once and compare to the pre-set thresholds. Decision rule: proceed to paper trading only if OOS profit factor and year-stability hold within tolerance of in-sample; abandon if OOS edge collapses (the Rosa / QQQ-OOS base-rate outcome).
Stage 3 — Benchmarks that would change the plan: if OOS per-trade edge < cost, or if the 2024–2026 sub-period is negative while earlier years carry the result, treat the effect as decayed and shelve it. If the FOMC/high-volatility label analysis shows edge concentrated there, consider a separate low-frequency FOMC-day strategy (accepting that it alone won't meet the 20/month floor).
Caveats

Nasdaq is the weakest documented vehicle: QQQ's 0.70% OOS R² (Gao et al.) and Mesfin's (2026) MNQ null result are direct warnings. The broad-S&P/SPY evidence is stronger than the Nasdaq-specific evidence; do not assume SPY Sharpe ratios transfer to MNQ.
Peer-reviewed disconfirmation exists: Rosa (2022) is a published, credible out-of-sample failure. This is not a settled "live" anomaly.
Move-magnitude figures are proxies: no peer-reviewed source reports the exact average last-30-minute Nasdaq move in points; the ~40–75-point estimate is reasoned from QQQ/U-shape intraday-volatility proxies and should be measured directly on your own data before relying on the cost-distance argument quantitatively.
Mechanism caveat: the gamma-hedging story predicts the effect strengthens with dealer short-gamma; the explosion of 0DTE options since 2022 could either amplify or distort the classic 15:30–16:00 window. This is untested in the published literature for current MNQ.
Definitional sensitivity: results depend on whether r1 is anchored to the prior close (Gao et al.) or the RTH open, and on exact exit timing relative to the 16:00 cash close versus the 16:15 futures settle. Lock these choices before testing.
Practitioner sources are not evidence: Zarattini, Aziz & Barbon (2024, Swiss Finance Institute Research Paper No. 24-97) report a SPY "noise-area" intraday strategy with "an annualized return of 19.6%, and a Sharpe Ratio of 1.33" (rising to 3.50 when VIX exceeds 40), and QuantifiedStrategies cites similar figures — but these use continuous intraday trend entries with dynamic trailing stops and heavy optimization, are distinct from the once-daily hold-to-close effect, and are not peer-reviewed. Treat them as inspiration, not confirmation. SSRN
